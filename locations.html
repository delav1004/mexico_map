Ensenada

Ensenada is a Mexican port city on the Pacific side of the Baja California peninsula. In the center is the port and the coastal area with the Malecón promenade, the fish market and the taco stands. The Museo de Historia and the Museo Histórico Regional de Ensenada present exhibits on the history and inhabitants of the region. Migratory gray whales visit its coastal waters. To the southwest of the city is the maritime geyser "La Bufadora".

Loreto

Loreto is a Mexican city on the Baja California peninsula, in the Gulf of California. Its colonial buildings include the Mission of Nuestra Señora de Loreto, a 17th-century church. Off the coast, the islands and waters of the Bahía de Loreto National Park are home to dolphins, whales, and pelicans. The city is sheltered by the mountains of the Sierra de la Giganta, with trails leading to prehistoric cave paintings.

Mazatlán

Mazatlán is a Mexican seaside resort on the Pacific coast in the state of Sinaloa. Sandy beaches line its 21 km long boardwalk, and the place is famous for deep-sea fishing. In its Centro Histórico, or Old Mazatlán, iconic 19th-century sites include the Ángela Peralta Theater and the towering Basilica of the Immaculate Conception. The trendy Zona Dorada district is known for its nightlife and hotels.

Monterrey

Monterrey, the capital of the state of Nuevo León in northeastern Mexico, is a sprawling industrial and business center surrounded by mountains. At the top is the Palacio del Obispado, an old Baroque construction with a regional museum. The imposing Cerro de la Silla, a mountain that teases adventurers with its steep hiking trails, reigns over the city. Its architecture stands out in the city, and it houses Mexico's most prestigious bookstore. In the center is the Macroplaza, a large green area of connected squares. This area houses the impressive Museum of Contemporary Art and the Faro de Comercio tower, which emits a green laser into the sky.

Peña de Bernal

59 kilometers from the city of Querétaro stands the imposing Peña de Bernal, a rock formation approximately 300 meters high. This monolith nestled in the Sierra Gorda of Queretaro is considered the third largest in the world, after the Rock of Gibraltar in Spain and the Sugar Loaf in Brazil. Those who practice rock climbing or mountaineering will find in the Peña de Bernal an earthly paradise. At the foot of the rock sits Villa de Bernal, a Magical Town with beautiful doorways, irregular cobbled streets, inns, restaurants, crafts, snack stalls, museums, and colorful houses.

Tula de Allende

Tula de Allende is in the Hidalgo region of Mexico. It is an archeological site of a city destroyed in the 12th century at some time between 1168 and 1179. Tula became the capital city following Teotihuacan. It is now a major tourist attraction in the state of Hidalgo. The famous columns resembling Aztec warriors and the pyramid ruins are the most visited part of the ruins.

Huasca de Ocampo

Huasca de Ocampo is a city in the central state of Hidalgo, Mexico. It is known for its ceramics and has narrow streets lined with craft shops. The 16th-century Church of San Juan Bautista honors the city's patron saint. Nearby are the Basaltic Prisms, a tall rock formation cascading from the San Antonio Dam. To the west is El Chico National Park, with waterfalls and forest trails.

Guanajuato

Guanajuato is a central state of Mexico, known as the cradle of the country's independence. Its capital, with the same name, houses the granary of the Alhóndiga de Granaditas, where the first battle against Spain in the 19th century took place. The former mining town is also famous for its steep cobblestone streets lined with colonial architecture and picturesque garden squares, such as the Jardín de la Unión. Many tourists and locals consider this city to be the most beautiful in Mexico. The city is built on very hilly ground, so virtually every point in the city is on a slant. The city has a network of underground tunnels that serve as roads making this place unique in the world.

Tequila

Tequila is a town in the state of Jalisco, in Mexico, and is located about 60 km from the city of Guadalajara. The name of Tequila comes from the Nahuatl word "Tecuilan" (Place of tributes). It is the head of the municipality of the same name. Tequila is known for giving tequila its name, being one of the territories where it is produced. Its primitive settlers were Chichimecas, Otomi, Toltecs and Nahuatlacas. The town was initially settled in a place called Teochichán or Techinchán: place of the almighty god or where snares and traps abound.

Paso de Cortés

Popocatepetl is currently a very active volcano with forested slopes and a large crater. It is the second-highest peak in Mexico, behind Pico de Orizaba and is generally considered the fifth-highest mountain in North America. Popocatepetl is Aztec for "Smoking Mountain"; also known as Popo. Popo and its neighbouring volcano Iztaccihuatl dominate the sky east of Amecameca. They are located less than 80 km from Mexico City and are separated from each other by ten miles of high grounds referred to as Paso de Cortes. When there is low volcanic activity, the Paso de Cortés can be reached either from the town of Amecameca by a winding paved road or from Santiago Xalitzintla, in Puebla. However, since 1994, access to the Popocatépetl volcano and the abandoned Tlamacas lodge has been limited.

What to to there?

Teotihuacán

Teotihuacán is a large Mexican archaeological complex northeast of Mexico City. Through the center of the place, which was once a flourishing pre-Columbian city, passes the Calzada de los Muertos. This unites the Temple of Quetzalcóatl, the Pyramid of the Moon and the Pyramid of the Sun. The last two have panoramic views from their tops. Artifacts at the on-site Museo de la Cultura Teotihuacana include pottery and bone work.

Mexico City

In Mexico City, life is as intense as its own urban layout. Within the same street it is possible to find the most sophisticated buildings coexisting with archaeological remains and traditional street food stalls. The city seamlessly intertwines tradition and modernity in just a few meters' distance. The ancient Tenochitlán, capital of the Aztecs, is today a magical, powerful, and vibrant city that overflows what it occupies and that, as its inhabitants say, "never ends". Mexico City is the largest city in North America, whose dimensions and population size make it one of the largest cities in the world. It is the main urban, social, political, academic, economic, tourist, artistic, business, and cultural nucleus of the Mexican nation, and the city has witnessed many significant events in the nation's history, making it a crucial part of the country's identity. Mexico City is, after London, the city in the world with the largest number of museums, where the rich pre-Columbian heritage of Mexico is preserved. More than 160 museums, 100 art galleries, 30 concert halls and a historic center that reflects the country's colonial history, make Mexico City one of the most relevant and tourist-important Latin American cultural enclaves that exist.

Mazunte

Mazunte is an old fishing village on the southern coast of Mexico, nestled in an exceptional natural setting. Mazunte has opted for sustainable tourism where visitors can enjoy tranquility and relaxation, but also excursions, water activities such as surfing, and dreamy sunsets. Since time immemorial, turtles have come to this paradisiacal place to lay their eggs, which is why the Mexican Turtle Center was created here. In this complex, research is conducted and the conservation of the numerous species of marine and land turtles that inhabit Mexico is promoted.

Cancún

Cancun is a city located on the Yucatan peninsula that borders the Caribbean Sea and is known for its beaches, numerous tourist centers, and nightlife. With its powdery white sand beaches, turquoise blue waters, and spectacular coral reefs that lie just off its shores, it's no surprise that Cancun is one of Mexico's most popular tourist destinations. Here, visitors can enjoy endless days at the beach, while having a refreshing drink. Some of the most beautiful beaches in the hotel zone are Playa Gaviota Azul and Playa Delfines, the latter is a great option for people looking for moderate or intense waves.

Palenque

Palenque is a pre-Hispanic city dating back to the 3rd century. It was one of the most notable cities of the Mayan world and also served as a ceremonial center thanks to Mayan patrons interested in demonstrating their power. It was one of the most important Mayan cities of its time, on par with Calakmul and Tikal. The current population is composed of indigenous peoples such as the Chol, Tzeltal, and Lacandon. Palenque is located in the heart of southeastern Mexico, in the northeast of the state of Chiapas, in an area of high tropical jungle where waterfalls and rivers abound.

Chichen Itzá

One of the Seven Wonders of the World, Chichen Itza, is a magnificent example of the advanced civilization of the Mayan people. It is located in the state of Yucatan, 128 km from Merida in the direction of Cancun, and receives more than one million tourists every year, making it the second most visited archaeological site in Mexico, after Teotihuacan. It was founded by Mayan groups between the years 325 and 550 AD. Around the year 800, the Toltecs invaded the area, causing the fusion of the two cultures. Thus, the city reached its maximum splendor and became the most powerful in the Yucatan Peninsula in the early 12th century.
